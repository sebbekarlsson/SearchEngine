At 450,295 square kilometres (173,860 sq mi), Sweden is the third-largest country in the European Union by area, with a total population of about 9.6 million.[1]
 Sweden has a low population density of 21 inhabitants per square kilometre (54 /sq mi) with the population mostly concentrated to the southern half of the country.
About 85% of the population live in urban areas.[10] Southern Sweden is predominantly agricultural, while the north is heavily forested.
